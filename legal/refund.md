## General Rule

All sales are final once the digital product has been delivered, made available for download, assigned to a license, or otherwise provided to the customer.

| Product state | Refund status |
| --- | --- |
| Delivered or downloaded | Generally non-refundable |

| Product not delivered due to verified system error | May be eligible |
| Duplicate purchase by mistake | May be eligible, subject to review |

## Possible Exceptions

CB Studios may review refund requests on a case-by-case basis. Approval is not guaranteed.

Possible exceptions may include:

- Duplicate purchases of the same product by the same customer.
- Verified delivery failure where access was never provided.
- A confirmed product defect that CB Studios cannot reasonably fix.
- A purchase made fraudulently through unauthorized account access.
- Any refund required by applicable law.

## Defective Products

If a product appears defective, the customer must contact CB Studios support and provide enough information to reproduce and investigate the issue.

| Required information | Examples |
| --- | --- |
| Product name | Resource name and version |
| Purchase proof | Order ID, transaction ID, Tebex ID, or license key |
| Server environment | Framework, dependencies, server artifact, database |
| Error details | Console logs, screenshots, reproduction steps |
| Modifications | Any changes made after delivery |

CB Studios will make a reasonable effort to provide troubleshooting, patches, documentation updates, or compatibility guidance for verified issues.

!> **Warning:** A refund may be denied if the product was modified in unsupported ways, installed incorrectly, used with missing dependencies, obtained from an unauthorized source, or used in violation of the license.

## Non-Refundable Situations

Refunds are generally not provided for:

- Change of mind after purchase.
- Failure to read product requirements or documentation.
- Incompatibility caused by unsupported third-party resources.
- Incorrect installation where support instructions were not followed.
- Unsupported custom server frameworks or heavily modified environments.
- Account sharing, license sharing, or unauthorized redistribution.
- Chargebacks, payment disputes, or policy violations.
